Paya Nihari

Hyderabadi Paya Nihari is a very popular breakfast recipe in Hyderabad, which is enjoyed mostly during the winter. It's name was originated from an Urdu word "Nahar" which means "Morning".

Khichdi Khatta

Khichdi-Khatta-Kheema is a prominent meal in Hyderabadi cuisine. It is usually prepared on a lazy weekend as a late morning brunch. It is best enjoyed with til or (sesame seeds) ki chutney.

Haleem

Haleem is a type of stew popular in the Middle East, Central Asia, and Pakistan. Although the dish varies from region to region, it optionally includes wheat or barley, meat and lentils. It is everybody's favourite in hyderabad during the holy month of ramadan.

Double Ka Meetha

Double Ka Meetha derives its name from ‘Double Roti’, a term used for bread during the British era. This bread pudding showcases the deliciousness of bread when it absorbs the richness of milk and cream and is flavoured with dry fruits.

Mutton Dalcha

Mutton Dalcha Recipe is yet another Hyderabadi cuisine often made with meat or mutton mixed in bottle gourd(kaddu) and dal(lentils). It is the most common dish made in hyderabad which has been passing on since the Nizam’s era. It is a popular lunch recipe!!

Bagaare Baingan

Bagaare baingan are made by deep frying young egg plants and then simmered in a gravy. Since they are deep fried they turn soft and mouth melting. When simmered in nutty and tangy gravy, they absorb all flavours and taste delicious. Peanut, sesame seeds, coconut are used to make the gravy nutty.

Dum Ka Murgh

Dum ka Murgh is also popularly known as Lagan ka Murgh in Hyderabad which is actually slow cooked marinated chicken in cashew and poppy gravy. The chicken is marinated in yoghurt and spices which gives a rich colour and texture to the gravy. It is served best with Roomali Roti or Naan.

Biryani

The authentic Hyderabadi biryani is meticulously prepared in the 'Kutchi' (raw) biryani style, in which raw rice and raw meat are cooked together in a 'handi' (earthen pot) with spices. It's distinct aroma, taste, tender meat, the Zaffran, everything gives it a distinguished appearance!
